Cybersecurity Fundamentals

Cybersecurity involves protecting computer systems, networks, and data from unauthorized access, disruption, or theft. Common attack vectors include phishing, where attackers trick users into revealing credentials or installing malicious software, and exploitation of unpatched software vulnerabilities that allow attackers to gain unauthorized access to systems. Defense in depth is a foundational principle in the field, meaning organizations layer multiple security controls, such as firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and access controls, so that the failure of any single defense does not lead to a full compromise. Encryption protects data both at rest and in transit, ensuring that intercepted or stolen data remains unreadable without the corresponding decryption key. As organizations have moved workloads to cloud infrastructure, security responsibilities are increasingly shared between the cloud provider, who secures the underlying infrastructure, and the customer, who must correctly configure access controls and monitor their own applications.

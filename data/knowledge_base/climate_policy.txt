Climate Policy

Climate policy encompasses the laws, regulations, and international agreements designed to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and adapt to the effects of a changing climate. Carbon pricing mechanisms, including carbon taxes and cap-and-trade systems, aim to make emissions costly so that businesses and consumers have a financial incentive to shift toward cleaner alternatives. International coordination has historically been difficult because the costs of reducing emissions are borne locally while the benefits of a stable climate are shared globally, creating an incentive for individual countries to under-invest in mitigation relative to the collective optimum. Many governments have also introduced subsidies and standards to accelerate the deployment of renewable energy, electric vehicles, and energy-efficient buildings. Adaptation policy, which focuses on managing unavoidable impacts such as sea level rise and more frequent extreme weather, has become an increasingly important complement to mitigation efforts as physical climate impacts have become more apparent.

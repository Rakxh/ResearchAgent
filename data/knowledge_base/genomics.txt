Genomics and Personalized Medicine

Genomics is the study of an organism's complete set of DNA, including the genes themselves and the way they interact with each other and the environment. The cost of sequencing a full human genome has fallen dramatically since the first genome was sequenced, from billions of dollars to a few hundred dollars today, making genomic data far more accessible for both research and clinical use. This has enabled the rise of personalized medicine, where treatment decisions can be informed by a patient's specific genetic profile, for example identifying which cancer therapies are most likely to be effective based on the genetic mutations present in a tumor. Large-scale genomic studies have also identified genetic variants associated with risk for many common diseases, though most of these variants individually have a small effect, meaning genetic risk is typically the product of many variants interacting with lifestyle and environmental factors. Privacy and data security are significant concerns in genomics, since genetic data is uniquely identifying and can reveal sensitive information about relatives as well as the individual tested.

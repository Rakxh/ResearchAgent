5G Wireless Networks

5G is the fifth generation of cellular network technology, designed to deliver substantially higher data speeds, lower latency, and greater device density than previous generations. Lower latency is particularly significant for applications that require near-instantaneous responses, such as remote control of industrial equipment or certain augmented reality applications, where delays of even a few hundred milliseconds can be noticeable or disruptive. 5G networks achieve higher capacity partly by using higher frequency radio bands, including millimeter wave spectrum, which can carry more data but travels shorter distances and is more easily blocked by buildings and foliage, requiring a denser network of smaller cell sites compared to earlier cellular generations. Network slicing is a key architectural feature of 5G, allowing operators to partition a single physical network into multiple virtual networks with different performance guarantees, so that, for example, an emergency services application can be guaranteed reliable bandwidth independent of general consumer traffic on the same infrastructure.

The Semiconductor Industry

The semiconductor industry designs and manufactures the integrated circuits that power virtually all modern electronics, from smartphones to data center servers. Chip manufacturing is extraordinarily capital intensive, requiring facilities called fabs that can cost tens of billions of dollars and rely on a small number of highly specialized equipment suppliers, particularly for the photolithography machines used to print circuit patterns at nanometer scale. This concentration has made semiconductor supply chains a significant geopolitical concern, since a small number of companies and countries control critical stages of production. Demand for advanced chips has been further amplified by the computational requirements of training and running large AI models, which rely on specialized processors optimized for the parallel matrix operations central to neural network computation. Many governments have introduced subsidies and industrial policy aimed at encouraging domestic semiconductor manufacturing capacity to reduce dependence on concentrated foreign supply chains.

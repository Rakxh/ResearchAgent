Financial Technology

Financial technology, or fintech, refers to the use of software and digital platforms to deliver financial services that were traditionally provided through banks and other established institutions. Mobile payment platforms have expanded financial access in regions with limited traditional banking infrastructure, allowing transactions to be conducted through basic smartphones without requiring a conventional bank account. Algorithmic lending platforms use alternative data sources and machine learning models to assess creditworthiness, potentially extending credit to borrowers who lack the credit history required by traditional underwriting methods, though this has also raised concerns about transparency and fairness in automated lending decisions. Robo-advisors provide automated investment management services at lower cost than traditional financial advisors by using algorithms to construct and rebalance diversified portfolios based on a client's stated risk tolerance and goals. Regulatory frameworks for fintech continue to evolve, as regulators work to balance encouraging innovation and competition against ensuring consumer protection and financial system stability.

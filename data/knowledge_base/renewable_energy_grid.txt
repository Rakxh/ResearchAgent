Integrating Renewable Energy into the Grid

Integrating large amounts of renewable energy, particularly solar and wind, into electricity grids presents technical challenges distinct from those of conventional power plants because renewable output varies with weather conditions rather than being dispatchable on demand. Grid operators address this variability through a combination of forecasting, which predicts renewable output hours or days in advance, and flexible resources such as natural gas plants, battery storage, and demand response programs that can quickly adjust supply or demand to match available generation. Battery storage has become increasingly important as costs have fallen, allowing excess renewable generation during periods of high output to be stored and discharged later when generation is low. Transmission infrastructure also needs to expand and adapt, since the best locations for wind and solar generation are often far from population centers, requiring new long-distance transmission lines to deliver that power where it is needed.

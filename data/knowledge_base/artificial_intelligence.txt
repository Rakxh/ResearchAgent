Artificial Intelligence

Artificial intelligence refers to computer systems capable of performing tasks that typically require human cognition, including language understanding, visual perception, and decision-making. The recent wave of progress has been driven largely by large language models trained on vast text corpora, which can generate fluent text, answer questions, and assist with coding by predicting likely continuations of a given input. Beyond language, AI techniques are widely used in image recognition, recommendation systems, and scientific research, where models can identify patterns in data too large or complex for manual analysis. A central challenge in deploying AI systems is ensuring their outputs are reliable and aligned with user intent, since models can produce plausible-sounding but incorrect information, a phenomenon often called hallucination. Organizations adopting AI increasingly pair model outputs with retrieval systems that ground responses in verified source documents, and with human review processes for high-stakes decisions.

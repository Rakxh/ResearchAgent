Biotechnology and Gene Editing

Biotechnology applies biological systems and organisms to develop products and technologies, spanning fields from agriculture to medicine. One of the most significant recent advances is CRISPR-based gene editing, which allows scientists to make precise changes to DNA sequences by guiding an enzyme to cut at a specific location in the genome. This technology has accelerated research into treatments for genetic diseases, since faulty genes can potentially be corrected directly rather than only managing symptoms. Beyond therapeutics, biotechnology techniques are used to engineer crops with improved yield or resistance to pests, and to produce proteins and other compounds using genetically modified microorganisms in industrial fermentation. Ethical and regulatory questions remain prominent in the field, particularly around editing genes in human embryos and the long-term ecological effects of releasing engineered organisms into the environment.
